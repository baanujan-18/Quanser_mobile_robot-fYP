 
1 PROBLEM SCOPE 
Problem Statement: Current patrol robots can navigate and detect objects, but they cannot justify their actions or 
prove they are following institutional policy. To meet EU AI Act and PDPA 2022 standards, enterprises now require 
on-device transparency and human oversight that functions without cloud dependency. 
In plain terms: today’s robots act but cannot explain themselves. Regulators now require a clear paper trail for every robot decision like 
a logged incident report generated on the robot itself, with no internet connection required.  
 
2 RESEARCH QUESTIONS 
• RQ1: Performance Safety Compliance Does the proposed PGDS significantly reduce critical policy violations 
compared to Rule-only and VLM-only baselines when deployed on edge hardware (Orin NX)? Metric: Macro-avg F1 
(weighted for False Negatives) across 200 labeled scenarios. 
• RQ2: Human-in-the-Loop Transparency Are the natural language justifications generated by PGDS accurate 
and accessible to non-technical operators? Metric: System Usability Scale (SUS)  68;  80% comprehension rate; 
and high Justification-Policy fidelity.

The Research Gap:  While prior works focus on functional planning, they fail in regulated environments. ACSAR -E 
addresses this by replacing probabilistic “soft prompts” with an executable rule layer, providing deterministic

components remain probabilistic by design. 
 
4 HYBRID SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE 
The ACSAR -E architecture separates probabilistic AI planning from deterministic policy enforcement to ensure  
verifiable safety on edge hardware. 
4.1 Policy-Constrained Decision Loop 
The core contribution is a Hybrid Reasoning Engine that acts as a mandatory filter between the VLM and the robot’s 
actuators: 
• VLM Planner: Generates candidate actions based on real-time environmental context and mission goals. 
• Symbolic Rule Engine: A deterministic filter that validates every candidate action against compiled institutional 
policy before execution. Justifications (J) are derived directly from the matched rule/policy path — not generated 
post hoc by the VLM. 
• Semantic Mapping: Utilizes RTAB-Map and Fast-LIO2 to annotate 3D zones, augmented by WiFi-derived 
occupancy heatmaps to identify presence in occluded areas (e.g., behind closed doors) for proactive policy lookup. 
Determinism scope and uncertainty behavior: The hybrid architecture provides deterministic guarantees at the 
enforcement layer only. When scene confidence c < 0.40 or sensor inputs conflict (e.g., LiDAR/camera disagreement), 
the system defaults to a safe hold posture and alerts the operator via TTS, rather than proceeding under uncertainty. 
Every such event is timestamped in the audit log to support post-hoc compliance review. 
4.2 Edge Reliability & Recovery 
To maintain “Zero-Cloud” autonomy on NVIDIA Orin NX hardware, the system integrates a Dynamic Recovery Node: 
• Failure Detection: If the IMU detects a tilt > 60° or a “Fall” state, the PGDS is immediately interrupted.

5 POLICY-GROUNDED DECISION SCHEMA (PGDS) 
5.1 Framework f (S, P) → (A, c, J) 
• Scene Graph (S): Multi-modal input combining 10 Hz YOLOv8n spatial data with WiFi-sensing trajectory vectors 
to represent both visible and occluded agents. 
• Policy (P): Executable YAML constraints. 
• Outputs: Action (A), Confidence (c), Justification (J). J is derived from the matched rule/policy clause — not 
post-hoc AI generation. 
5.2 Safety & Latency (RBSL)  
A 200 Hz C++ gatekeeper ensures: 
• Action Masking: Overrides if c < 0.40; defaults to safe hold posture. 
• Real-time Guard: < 0.4 s first-token latency; 3 s emergency stop trigger.

6 D1 Arm Whole-Body Control (WBC) Integration 
The integration of the Unitree D1 arm with the Go2 quadruped enables physical policy enforcement through a unified 
Whole-Body Control (WBC) strategy. This allows for precise manipulation and interaction unavailable to standard 
wheeled platforms. 
6.1 Operational Capabilities 
• Active Manipulation: Door handle operation with a force-controlled grasp (5-15 N torque limit). 
• Hazard Response: Arm-deployed physical markers at policy-violation sites. 
• Visual Servoing: Access card retrieval via D435i point cloud; arm re-plans if lever pose error exceeds 5 mm. 
6.2 Whole-Body Control Strategy 
To maintain equilibrium during high-torque tasks, the system coordinates the arm and base as a single entity: 
Table 2: WBC Parameters for D1 Arm Integration 
Feature Technical Implementation 
Base Stability Transitions to a four-point low-stance before arm activation to maximize the support polygon. 
Precision Nav2 maintains base stationarity within ±1 cm via closed-loop foot placement. 
Wrench Compensation Reaction forces from arm operation are fed back to the locomotion controller to neutralize body sway. 
Abort Logic RBSL monitors joint torques at 200 Hz; immediate stow-and-halt if unexpected resistance is detected. 
6.3 Software Stack 
The architecture utilizes MoveIt2 over ROS2 interfaced with the Unitree Arm SDK. Arm motion is constrained to 
stationary approaches, remaining stowed during all patrol motion or when velocity v > 0.3 m/s.

10. Deployment Platform (Unitree Go2 Edu) 
Locomotion: Quadrupedal stability for 0.7 m corridors and thresholds (> 25°); active compliance on contact. 
Versatility: YAML-based policies for Office (Privacy), Logistics (Hazmat/PPE), and Healthcare (HIPAA). 
Thermal Degradation Modes: To support sustained real -world operation, the system implements three graduated 
modes: (1) Normal: all modules active, VLM invoked on scene-change events; (2) Reduced (Orin NX > 78°C)  VLM 
invocation rate halved, non -critical logging suspended; (3) Safety-only (> 85°C) : VLM suspended, RBSL and Nav2 
remain fully active, operator alerted via TTS. Temperature and throttle state are logged continuously as runtime metrics. 
Like a car’s temperature warning: the robot scales back AI processing when hot but safety rules and navigation stay fully act ive at all 
times. 
 
11. Simulation-to-Real Validation Strategy 
 
Digital Twin Development: A high-fidelity digital twin of the deployment environment will be developed using NVIDIA Isaac 
Sim to enable controlled testing and validation. 
 
Wi-Fi Sensing Validation: An RF ray-tracing extension will be integrated to simulate non-line-of-sight (NLOS) agent 
movement, enabling rigorous stress testing of policy (P) constraints prior to real-world deployment. 
 
Sim-to-Real Risk Mitigation: A 5-pair matched-scenario methodology will be employed to systematically compare Wi-Fi 
signal processor performance between the simulated environment (Isaac Sim) and the physical Unitree Go2 Edu platform.
